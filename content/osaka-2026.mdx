## <span id="kansai-airport">[Kansai Airport](#kansai-airport)</span>

We landed at Kansai Airport in the late afternoon—pretty efficient as usual. The airport has a train straight to Namba, which took around 45 minutes, and we used that. Our hotel was in the Semba area, and we checked in early evening.

After settling in, we went for dinner nearby, then walked to Dontonbori to see the Glico sign.

## <span id="katsuoji-temple">[Katsuoji Temple](#katsuoji-temple)</span>

## <span id="osaka-castle-umeda-sky">[Osaka Castle & Umeda Sky](#osaka-castle-umeda-sky)</span>

On Sunday we visited Osaka Castle and Umeda Sky. The castle was just a station away from Sakaisuji-hommachi Station near our hotel, with about a 15-minute walk to reach the actual castle. The castle was quite enormous IRL.

## <span id="kyoto">[Kyoto](#kyoto)</span>

Kyoto, about an hour away via the Keihan line, offered us an entirely different side of Japan. It felt noticeably more laid-back than Tokyo or even Osaka. Nearly every meal we had here was outstanding. I also noticed that some stations had just a few passengers waiting for the next train, unlike the crowds in Tokyo or Osaka.

We went for the glass slide in the tower and spent some time in Shinsekai street. I feel like food and some items were slightly pricier here than what we got in other areas of Osaka. However, night time here was awesome—the place lit up warmly.

## <span id="usj">[Universal Studios Japan](#usj)</span>

We really had a great time here. In retrospect, I enjoyed it better than <SimpleLink href="/writing/tokyo-2024#disneysea">Tokyo Disney Sea</SimpleLink>. Our commute was really nice as well—though it was 2 train rides, it was very easy thanks to their thoughtful train system. I remember being very cold that day, or I just didn't dress properly for the weather. However, the establishments inside were warm so it was all good. I really enjoyed the Mario Kart ride with the augmented experience on the glasses you put on, and my wife enjoyed the roller coaster ride around the park.

## <span id="rokko-narra">[Rokko & Nara](#rokko-narra)</span>

We hit two different spots in one day, booked the whole thing through Klook and it worked out smoothly. First stop was Rokko Snow Park, where we finally got to experience snow and the slopes. After that, we headed to Nara Park and finally got to see the famous bowing deer up close. They really do bow when you offer them the crackers, it was as charming as everyone says.

## <span id="uji">[Uji](#uji)</span>

A short train ride from Kyoto, Uji is the matcha capital of Japan, and it shows. We stopped at a tea house for proper matcha and sweets, and the quality was noticeably different from anywhere else. The main draw for us was Byōdō-in Temple, the real-life building you see on the 10 yen coin. The temple grounds were peaceful, and the whole town had that quiet, old-Japan vibe.

## <span id="thoughts">[Thoughts](#thoughts)</span>

It seems like we enjoyed Osaka better than Tokyo. The Kansai region felt a bit more relaxed, Kyoto's quieter streets, the warmth of Shinsekai at night, and even the bowing deer in Nara had a different pace than the bustle we remembered from the capital. The food was consistently excellent, and the train system made getting around effortless. Winter in Japan hit different this time. Snow at Rokko, matcha in Uji, and Super Nintendo World under the cold sky. Eleven days flew by.
