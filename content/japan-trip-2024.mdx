## <span id="shinjuku">[Shinjuku](#shinjuku)</span>

Arrived at Narita Airport into 4–7°C—a sharp contrast. Picked up pocket Wi‑Fi and boarded the pre‑booked Airport Limousine Bus to Shinjuku (4 p.m. departure). Reached Kabukicho Tower around 5 p.m. as the sun set, then walked to our hotel.

After settling in, we headed back to Kabukicho for dinner. Maps directed us to a gyukatsu spot that wasn’t there, so we had ramen instead—our first meal in Tokyo. 😅 Stopped at a konbini for breakfast supplies before our early Mt. Fuji trip. 🗻

## <span id="mt-fuji">[Mt. Fuji](#mt-fuji)</span>

The second day was all about capturing iconic views of Mt. Fuji. We booked a Klook itinerary with these stops:

- **Lake Kawaguchi**: Biting cold, but we couldn't resist trying the lavender ice cream.
- **Chureito Pagoda**: We trekked up to the pagoda, stunning view of Mt. Fuji in full display.
- **Oshino Hakkai**: A charming village with several ponds flowing almost like a river.
- **Lawson Store**: Yes, the famous Lawson store Mt. Fuji in the background.
- **Clock shop**: quiet, with very few people. Just us tourists and the occasional traffic enforcer.

## <span id="disneysea">[Disney Sea](#disneysea)</span>

DisneySea was a highlight—27,000 steps tested our stamina. We used the Disney Resort app to manage queues and explore shops all day, but barely scratched the surface.

We traveled by train from Shinjuku with three transfers and paper tickets—a great way to learn how the Tokyo subway system works.

![27k steps](https://yzft9j8wzzlydetx.public.blob.vercel-storage.com/blog/japan-trip-2024/IMG_0910-YjsdV8UVd06cquZFAe8AbEZ4fUvpiq.jpg "Apple watch says 27k steps!")

## <span id="shibuya-sky">[Shibuya Sky](#shibuya-sky)</span>

Shibuya Sky offered panoramic views—so sunny I wished for sunglasses. In the same building, we found a souvenir shop and had a teppan‑yaki course. Then we visited the Hachiko statue and experienced the Shibuya Scramble Crossing. We continued to Harajuku for shopping and ended with Ichiran ramen—rich flavors and the chain’s unique solitary dining experience.

![Shibuya Sky Labs](https://yzft9j8wzzlydetx.public.blob.vercel-storage.com/blog/japan-trip-2024/IMG_1105-bb2ZnMEu8CmA3Ty5vqO3ImwqlnMzlo.jpg "Shibuya Sky Labs")

## <span id="shopping-and-local">[Shopping and Local](#shopping-and-local)</span>

We dove into local shopping culture at Don Quijote and Bic Camera, picking up souvenirs, charger adapters, and a hefty 4TB SSD—all tax-free.
The organization of Tokyo was evident; from clean streets to polite locals, everything ran like clockwork.

![Shrine](https://yzft9j8wzzlydetx.public.blob.vercel-storage.com/blog/japan-trip-2024/IMG_1249-tS4DaSYs8sFdrlKcY05MZPuOrcxVbb.jpg "Shrine")

## <span id="departure">[Departure](#departure)</span>

We had an early start for our late‑morning flight from Narita. The private transfer from Shinjuku was smooth and hassle‑free. We used our remaining time for last‑minute snacks and souvenirs, then boarded our flight home with bags packed and memories.

## <span id="thoughts">[Thoughts](#thoughts)</span>

Tokyo—and Japan as a whole—is a place where nearly everything just makes sense.
From thoughtful konbini packaging to pristine streets and deep respect ingrained in daily interactions, these are what we'll miss most.
Navigating the train system without IC cards added an element of adventure, giving us a newfound appreciation for Japanese public transport efficiency.

The sounds of Don Quijote and Bic Camera, paired with the distinct bird-like tones at traffic lights, are melodies that will stay with us. Even now, back home, we can still hear those Tokyo sounds and picture the seamless blend of tradition and modernity.

Until next time, Japan!
