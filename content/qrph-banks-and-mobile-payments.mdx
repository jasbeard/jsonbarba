Today, QRph failed me at the grocery store.

I had been using QRph for payments without issues for months. But when I completed a payment, the cashier showed me their terminal report—no transaction appeared, despite my confirmation.

I returned the items and requested proof of the missing transaction. Through my bank's app, customer service responded three hours later: the money was sent but floating, and investigation could take 3–4 weeks.

Later, I realized the cashier had pressed "Confirm payment" seconds before I finalized the transaction. Could a simple timing mismatch cause funds to disappear into limbo?

If so, this is a critical flaw for a system aiming for widespread adoption. It could happen daily if cashiers aren't meticulous.

Was it the cashier's mistake, a system limitation, or both?

<br />

## <span id="mitigation">[Mitigation](#mitigation)</span>

Three strategies could prevent this:

- **Enhanced Transaction Synchronization** — Align confirmation between customer action and cashier terminal to prevent premature confirmations.

- **Robust Error Handling** — Prompt for reconfirmation or alert both parties when discrepancies or premature confirmations occur.

- **Educational Initiatives** — Train cashiers and users about timing issues and prevention.

<br />

## <span id="awareness">[Awareness](#awareness)</span>

Until these improvements are implemented, ensure cashiers wait until you've completed your transaction before pressing "Confirm Payment."

<br />

## <span id="conclusion">[Conclusion](#conclusion)</span>

Three weeks later, the money was credited back. But the experience exposed a critical weakness in QRph's design—one that shouldn't exist in a payment system built for scale.
