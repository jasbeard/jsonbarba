## <span id="hanoi-to-sapa">[Hanoi to Sapa](#hanoi-to-sapa)</span>

We touched down at Noi Bai Airport, set up our eSIM for connectivity, grabbed a snack, then waited about an hour for our sleeper bus to Sapa. It was a long 4-hour ride with enough space to lie down and nap. After two short stops, the bus line provided a van transfer to our hotel. We checked in, dropped our bags, and immediately headed out in search of our first bowl of pho.

## <span id="first-pho">[First Pho](#first-pho)</span>

My wife had a list of places to visit, but we didn't know their exact locations. I used Google Maps to find our first pho at Paradise Restaurant Sapa. The rich, aromatic broth, tender beef slices, fresh herbs, and perfectly cooked noodles were everything we hoped for—not just food, but an introduction to Vietnamese culture.

![Pho](https://1fjnp8gfmpn2zccc.public.blob.vercel-storage.com/vietnam-2025/IMG_6110.jpeg "First bowl of pho in Sapa")

## <span id="sapa-exploration">[Sapa Exploration](#sapa-exploration)</span>

Sapa itself is a charming mountain town. We spent our first day exploring the local market, where Hmong and Dao women in traditional colorful attire sold handmade textiles and local crafts. The fog would roll in and out, revealing glimpses of the surrounding mountains.

## <span id="fansipan">[Fansipan](#fansipan)</span>

We conquered Fansipan, Vietnam's highest peak at 3,143 meters, via cable car rising above terraced fields and into the clouds. At the summit, we explored the temple complex and viewpoints, spending hours taking in breathtaking panoramic views and absorbing the accomplishment of reaching the "Roof of Indochina."

![Fansipan Summit](https://1fjnp8gfmpn2zccc.public.blob.vercel-storage.com/vietnam-2025/fansipan.jpg "At the summit of Fansipan")

## <span id="alpine-coaster">[Alpine Coaster](#alpine-coaster)</span>

In the afternoon, we headed back down and tried the alpine coaster—a fun mini roller coaster without going upside down, on a cart-style ride.

![Alpine Coaster](https://1fjnp8gfmpn2zccc.public.blob.vercel-storage.com/vietnam-2025/IMG_5994.jpeg "Alpine Coaster")

We returned to Hanoi for few more days of shopping, sightseeing, and local cuisine. Some photos are on the <ExternalLink href="/pictorial">pictorial</ExternalLink> page if you'd like to see them.

## <span id="thoughts">[Thoughts](#thoughts)</span>

I'm bullish on Vietnam as a developing country. We plan to return to see other cities like Ho Chi Minh City. I really liked their tea—I bought three 100g packs of loose leaf and wish I'd bought more.
